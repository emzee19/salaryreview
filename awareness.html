What just happened

You received a simulated phishing email designed to look like an official HR Salary Review notification. The link pointed to a fake login portal mimicking your company's internal system. Had this been a real attack, your credentials could have been stolen and used to compromise your account — and potentially the entire organisation's systems.

Red flags in that email
01
Suspicious sender domain The "From" address was subtly different from your real company domain — a classic spoofing technique.
02
Manufactured urgency Phrases like "expires in 48 hours" or "access before Friday" are pressure tactics to stop you thinking clearly.
03
Credential request via link Legitimate HR portals do not ask you to re-enter your password through a link in an email.
04
No prior official announcement Real company-wide exercises or portals are typically announced through official internal channels first.
What to do next time
🔍
Inspect the sender address carefully. Hover over — or tap — the "From" name to see the full email address before engaging.
🚫
Never enter your credentials via an emailed link. Navigate directly to internal portals by typing the URL yourself.
📣
Report suspicious emails immediately. Forward to your IT team or use the "Report Phishing" button in your mail client.
🔑
If in doubt, change your password. Even in a simulation, treat it as real and reset credentials as a precaution.
